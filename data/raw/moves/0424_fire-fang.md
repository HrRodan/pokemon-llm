## Identity & Mechanics
**Fire Fang** is a Physical Fire-type move introduced in Generation IV (Sinnoh).
* **Description:** The user ignites its teeth with intense thermal energy and snaps shut on the target with a searing bite. This aggressive maneuver combines physical piercing force with extreme heat, potentially leaving the opponent with lasting burns or causing them to recoil in pain.
* **Effect:** Inflicts damage and has two independent secondary effects: a 10% chance to Burn the target and a 10% chance to cause the target to Flinch.
* **Secondary Effect:** 10% chance to Burn; 10% chance to Flinch.
* **Targeting:** Single Target.

## Competitive Profile
* **Stats:** Power 65, Accuracy 95%, PP 15.
* **Role:** Utility Coverage / Ability-Dependent STAB.
* **Usage:** While its base power is relatively low, Fire Fang serves as a vital **Coverage** option for physical attackers to hit Steel-types. It is often compared to *Fire Punch* (75 Power / 100 Acc) or *Flare Blitz* (120 Power / Recoil). It is the preferred choice for Pokémon with the **Strong Jaw** ability, which boosts the move's power by 50%, elevating it to a more formidable 97.5 base power. It deals **Super Effective** damage to **Grass, Ice, Bug, and Steel** types.
* **Attributes:** This is a **Contact** move and a **Bite-based** move (affected by Strong Jaw).

## Distribution & Notable Users
It has a Wide distribution (136 learners), primarily found among predatory or beast-like Pokémon.
* **Prominent Users:** Commonly utilized by **Arcanine**, **Incineroar**, **Gouging Fire**, and **Luxray**.
* **Coverage Utility:** Frequently seen on non-Fire Pokémon like **Garchomp**, **Salamence**, and **Tyranitar** to lure in and punish **Steel-type** checks such as Corviknight, Ferrothorn, or Scizor.
* **Acquisition:** Widely available as a **TM** or **TR** in modern titles, including Pokémon Sword/Shield and Scarlet/Violet.
