## Identity & Mechanics
**Play Rough** is a physical Fairy-type move introduced in Generation VI (Kalos).
* **Description:** The user focuses intense Fairy-type energy and engages the opponent in a rough-and-tumble physical melee. Despite its whimsical name, the move involves aggressive wrestling and physical confrontation, manifesting as a cloud of dust or a flurry of pink energy impacts as the user "plays" with the target.
* **Effect:** Inflicts physical damage to a single target.
* **Secondary Effect:** 10% chance to lower the target’s Attack stat by one stage.
* **Targeting:** Selected single opponent.

## Competitive Profile
* **Stats:** Power 90, Accuracy 90%, PP 10 (Max 16).
* **Role:** Reliable STAB / Physical Wallbreaker.
* **Usage:** Play Rough serves as the primary—and often only—viable Physical Fairy-type STAB move for many attackers. While its 90% accuracy can lead to "unfortunate misses" in critical moments, its solid 90 base power makes it a staple. It is the physical counterpart to Moonblast. It deals **Super Effective** damage to **Dragon, Dark, and Fighting** types, making it an essential tool for "Dragon-slaying" and breaking through Dark-type walls.
* **Attributes:** This is a **Contact** move, meaning it triggers effects like Rocky Helmet, Iron Barbs, or Rough Skin.

## Distribution & Notable Users
It has a **Wide** distribution (204 learners), making it one of the most accessible Fairy-type moves for physical attackers.
* **Prominent Users:** 
    * **Azumarill:** Utilizing the *Huge Power* ability, Azumarill uses Play Rough as its primary nuke to threaten massive damage.
    * **Mimikyu:** Often pairs this move with *Swords Dance* to sweep teams after its *Disguise* takes a hit.
    * **Zacian (Crowned):** In Ubers/VGC, this is Zacian's secondary STAB move to handle Dragon-types that resist its Steel attacks.
    * **Tinkaton:** Relies on Play Rough as a consistent damage option when *Gigaton Hammer* is on cooldown.
* **Coverage Utility:** Frequently seen on non-Fairy Pokémon such as **Great Tusk**, **Iron Hands**, and **Meowscarada** to provide crucial coverage against opposing Dark and Fighting-type threats.
* **Acquisition:** It is widely available as **TM127** in Generation IX and was **TR90** in Generation VIII, ensuring it is a staple in most competitive movepools.
