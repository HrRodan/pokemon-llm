## Identity & Mechanics
**Nasty Plot** is a Status Dark-type move introduced in Generation IV (Sinnoh).
* **Description:** The user stimulates its brain by focusing on wicked thoughts and malicious schemes, effectively overclocking its mental capabilities. This surge of dark ingenuity results in a dramatic increase in the user's intellectual power and magical output.
* **Effect:** Sharply raises the user's Special Attack stat by two stages (+100% / 2x multiplier). 
* **Secondary Effect:** None.
* **Targeting:** Affects the user only.

## Competitive Profile
* **Stats:** Power — , Accuracy —%, PP 20 (Max 32).
* **Role:** Setup Sweeper / Special Wallbreaker.
* **Usage:** Nasty Plot is the Special equivalent of Swords Dance. It is a cornerstone of offensive competitive play, allowing a Pokémon to double its damage output in a single turn. It is often preferred over *Calm Mind* on fast, frail sweepers who prioritize immediate, overwhelming power over incremental bulk. In the current meta, it is essential for breaking through dedicated Special Walls like Blissey or Clodsire.
* **Attributes:** As a Status move, it is blocked by **Taunt**. Because it is a Dark-type move, it cannot be redirected or blocked by standard defensive abilities like Lightning Rod or Storm Drain.

## Distribution & Notable Users
It has a very Wide distribution (211 learners), making it one of the most accessible setup moves for Special Attackers.
* **Prominent Users:** Commonly utilized by **Gholdengo**, **Chi-Yu**, **Darkrai**, and **Togekiss**. In Generation 9, Gholdengo uses Nasty Plot to become an unblockable offensive threat behind its "Good as Gold" ability.
* **Coverage Utility:** Frequently seen on mixed attackers or non-Dark types like **Lucario**, **Infernape**, and **Mew** to pivot into a Special Sweeping role and surprise physical walls. 
* **Acquisition:** Widely available as a **TM** (Technical Machine) or **TR** (Technical Record) in most modern generations, ensuring easy access for most competitive builds.
