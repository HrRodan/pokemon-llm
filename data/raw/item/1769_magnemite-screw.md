## Identity & Utility
**Magnemite Screw** is a **TM Material** introduced in **Generation IX** (Pokémon Scarlet & Violet).
* **Description:** This item represents a mechanical component shed or dropped by the Magnemite evolutionary line. It is a **Consumable Crafting Material** specifically used in the Paldea region's TM Machine system. It cannot be held by Pokémon in battle or used directly as a medicine.
* **Cost:** 80 Poké Dollars (**Cheap**). While it can be sold for a small amount of currency, its utility as a crafting reagent far outweighs its sell value.

## Effect & Mechanics
* **Primary Effect:** Used as a localized currency/material at **TM Machines** located at Pokémon Centers. It is combined with League Points (LP) to create specific Technical Machines.
* **Battle Attributes:**
    * **Fling Power:** N/A (This item cannot be held; therefore, it cannot be used with the move Fling).
    * **Competitive Use:** **Indirect Utility.** While the screw provides no tactical advantage in a fight, it is a prerequisite for teaching essential competitive moves. It is a required ingredient for crafting high-usage TMs such as **TM048 (Volt Switch)**, **TM082 (Thunder Wave)**, **TM093 (Flash Cannon)**, and **TM166 (Thunder)**.
* **Evolution/Interaction:** This item does not trigger evolution. It is categorized strictly under the "TM Materials" bag pocket.

## Acquisition & Lore
* **Availability:** Dropped by wild Pokémon upon defeat or capture. It is a common resource for trainers exploring rocky, mountainous, or industrial biomes where Electric/Steel types congregate.
* **Wild Held Item:** It is not a held item, but a guaranteed or highly likely drop from the following species:
    * **Magnemite**
    * **Magneton**
    * **Magnezone**
* **Farming Context:** Trainers looking to optimize their team's movesets often farm Magnemite Screws in areas like **East Province (Area Two)** or the **Glaseado Mountain** foothills, where Magnemite appear frequently.
