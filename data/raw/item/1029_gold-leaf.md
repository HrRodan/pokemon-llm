## Identity & Utility
**Gold Leaf** is a **Loot** item introduced in **Generation VII** (specifically appearing in the *Let's Go, Pikachu!* and *Let's Go, Eevee!* era).
* **Description:** A mysterious, metallic leaf of unknown origin. It is a **Consumable/Sellable** item that **cannot** be purchased from standard shops; it is intended to be found and sold to vendors.
* **Cost:** 1,000 Poké Dollars (**Moderate Value Loot**; typically sells for 500 Poké Dollars).

## Effect & Mechanics
* **Primary Effect:** The Gold Leaf serves no functional purpose in battle, healing, or Pokémon training. Its sole utility is as a "high-value" item to be exchanged for currency at Poké Marts.
* **Battle Attributes:**
    * **Fling Power:** 30 (Low damage; no secondary status effects).
    * **Competitive Use:** **Non-existent.** It is a "vendor trash" item used exclusively for progressing the player's economy during a playthrough. It lacks any held-item benefits.
* **Evolution/Interaction:** This item does not trigger any evolutions and does not interact with specific Pokémon abilities (except for potentially being found via the **Pickup** mechanic in certain games).

## Acquisition & Lore
* **Availability:** In the *Let's Go* series, this item is primarily discovered by the player's **Partner Pokémon** (Pikachu or Eevee) or a following Pokémon. They may find it while scouring bushes or interacting with specific environmental points in various routes.
* **Wild Held Item:** Not currently held by wild Pokémon according to the provided data. Historically, similar "Leaf" items were associated with specific time-based events or following Pokémon mechanics, reinforcing its status as a reward for exploration and bonding with a Pokémon.
