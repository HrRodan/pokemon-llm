## Identity & Utility
**TM119 (Energy Ball)** is a **Technical Machine** categorized under **All-Machines**, specifically associated with the Generation IX titles, *Pokémon Scarlet & Violet* and the *Indigo Disk* expansion.

* **Description:** A move-teaching device that imparts the move **Energy Ball** to a compatible Pokémon. In the Generation IX context, TMs are single-use consumables that can be reproduced at a TM Machine using League Points (LP) and specific Pokémon materials.
* **Cost:** 0 Poké Dollars (**Not for sale**). It is acquired via exploration, quest rewards, or crafting rather than direct purchase with currency.

## Effect & Mechanics
* **Primary Effect:** Teaches the move **Energy Ball**. Energy Ball is a powerful Grass-type Special move with a base power of 90 and 100% accuracy. It has a 10% chance to lower the target's Special Defense stat by one stage.
* **Battle Attributes:**
    * **Fling Power:** N/A (Technical Machines cannot be effectively used with Fling in recent generations).
    * **Competitive Use:** **High/Staple.** Energy Ball is the "gold standard" for Special Grass-type coverage. It is frequently used by Special Sweepers (like Flutter Mane or Iron Valiant) for coverage against Water, Ground, and Rock types, or as a reliable STAB (Same Type Attack Bonus) move for Grass-type specialists.
* **Evolution/Interaction:** This item does not trigger evolution. It is strictly a teaching tool.

## Acquisition & Lore
* **Availability:** 
    * **Crafting:** In *Scarlet & Violet*, this TM can be crafted at any Pokémon Center TM Machine. The recipe typically requires **8,000 LP**, **Deerling Hooves (x3)**, and **Bramblin Twigs (x3)**.
    * **Exploration:** Often found as a localized item pick-up in the overworld (specifically in the North Province or within the Terarium of Blueberry Academy).
* **Wild Held Item:** Not held by wild Pokémon. Materials required to craft it are gathered by defeating or catching wild **Deerling**, **Sawsbuck**, **Bramblin**, or **Bramblghast**.
