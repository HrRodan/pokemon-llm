## Identity & Utility
**Data Card 26** is a **Data Card** (Key Item variant) introduced in **Generation IV** (specifically *Pokémon HeartGold and SoulSilver*).
* **Description:** A specialized digital record-keeper used within the Pokéathlon facilities. It archives specific performance metrics—specifically the frequency of trainer instructions—allowing players to track their progress and milestones. It is a **Key Item** utility that **cannot** be bought with standard currency or sold.
* **Cost:** 0 Poké Dollars (Not for sale; acquired via special points).

## Effect & Mechanics
* **Primary Effect:** Records and displays the total number of times the Trainer has "instructed" their Pokémon during Pokéathlon events. It serves as a progress tracker for completionists and those aiming to unlock higher-tier trophies in the Pokéathlon Dome.
* **Battle Attributes:**
    * **Fling Power:** N/A (This item cannot be held or used in battle; it resides in the Key Items pocket).
    * **Competitive Use:** **None.** This item has no impact on combat mechanics, IVs, EVs, or move-sets. It is strictly for flavor and mini-game record-keeping.
* **Evolution/Interaction:** Does not trigger evolution or interact with any Pokémon abilities (such as Pickup or Klutz).

## Acquisition & Lore
* **Availability:** Exclusive to the **Pokéathlon Dome** in the Johto region (*HeartGold/SoulSilver*). These cards are typically purchased at the Athlete Shop using **Athlete Points (AP)** earned by participating in various athletic competitions.
* **Wild Held Item:** This item is never held by wild Pokémon; it is a unique technological tool provided to registered athletes in the Johto circuit. 
* **Context:** Data Card 26 is one of 27 collectible cards that document every possible statistic of a player's Pokéathlon career, representing the data-driven side of Pokémon sports.
