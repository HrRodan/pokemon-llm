## Identity & Utility
**TM164 (Brave Bird)** is a **Technical Machine** introduced in **Generation IX (Scarlet & Violet)**.
* **Description:** A teaching device used to grant a Pokémon the move **Brave Bird**. In Generation IX, TMs are single-use consumable items that can be crafted at a TM Machine. It is categorized as a high-tier offensive machine.
* **Cost:** 0 Poké Dollars (Not for sale; acquired through crafting or exploration).

## Effect & Mechanics
* **Primary Effect:** Teaches the move **Brave Bird** to a compatible Pokémon. 
    * **Move Detail:** Brave Bird is a Physical Flying-type move with **120 Base Power** and **100% Accuracy**. The user takes recoil damage equal to 1/3 of the damage dealt to the target.
* **Battle Attributes:**
    * **Fling Power:** 30 (Standard for most Technical Machines).
    * **Competitive Use:** **High Tier / Essential.** Brave Bird is the gold standard for Physical Flying-type attackers (e.g., Talonflame, Corviknight, Staraptor). It is a staple in both VGC and Smogon formats for its high immediate damage output, often paired with items like Choice Band or Life Orb to maximize "wall-breaking" potential.
* **Evolution/Interaction:** Does not trigger evolution. It is primarily used to expand the offensive coverage of Flying-type Pokémon or those with high physical Attack stats.

## Acquisition & Lore
* **Availability:** In *Pokémon Scarlet & Violet*, this TM can be found in the overworld (notably in the North Province) or unlocked for crafting at the TM Machine after defeating specific Team Star bases or progressing through the Indigo Disk DLC.
* **Crafting Requirements:** To craft TM164, the player typically requires:
    * **12,000 LP** (League Points)
    * **5x Rufflet Feather**
    * **3x Starly Feather**
    * **3x Rookidee Feather**
* **Wild Held Item:** Not found on wild Pokémon. It must be crafted using materials gathered from wild Flying-type Pokémon via the Let's Go mechanic or traditional battles.
