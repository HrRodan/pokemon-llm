## Identity & Utility
**TM127 (Play Rough)** is a **Technical Machine (TM)** introduced in its current iteration during **Generation IX (Pokémon Scarlet & Violet: The Indigo Disk)**.
* **Description:** This machine is used to teach the move **Play Rough** to compatible Pokémon. As of Generation IX, TMs are consumable items that can be crafted repeatedly at any TM Machine found at Pokémon Centers. It represents a high-tier offensive tool for Physical attackers.
* **Cost:** 0 Poké Dollars (Not for sale). In Gen IX, it is acquired via exploration or crafted using **10,000 LP**, **5× Fidough Fur**, and **3× Dachsbun Fur**.

## Effect & Mechanics
* **Primary Effect:** Teaches the move **Play Rough**. 
    * **Move Profile:** A Physical Fairy-type move with **90 Base Power** and **90% Accuracy**. 
    * **Secondary Effect:** Has a 10% chance to lower the target's Attack stat by one stage.
* **Battle Attributes:**
    * **Fling Power:** N/A (TMs generally cannot be used with the move Fling in current generations).
    * **Competitive Use:** **High-Tier Staple.** Play Rough is the premier Physical Fairy-type STAB (Same Type Attack Bonus) move. It is essential for physical Fairy types like **Azumarill**, **Mimikyu**, **Zacian-Crowned**, and **Iron Valiant**. It provides crucial coverage against Dragon, Fighting, and Dark-type threats in both VGC and Smogon formats.
* **Evolution/Interaction:** Does not trigger evolution. However, it is a vital component of the "TM Machine" economy in the Paldea and Blueberry Academy regions.

## Acquisition & Lore
* **Availability:** Specifically associated with the **Terarium** in the Blueberry Academy (The Indigo Disk DLC). It can be found as a collectible item on the ground in the Coastal Biome or unlocked for crafting after defeating specific trainers or progressing through the DLC story.
* **Wild Held Item:** Not held by wild Pokémon. Materials required for its creation are gathered by defeating or catching members of the **Fidough** evolutionary line.
