## Identity & Utility
**TM101 (Power Gem)** is a **Technical Machine (TM)** categorized under **All-Machines**, specifically identified within the **Generation 9 (Scarlet & Violet)** data structure.
* **Description:** A move-teaching tool used to provide a Pokémon with the move **Power Gem**. In the context of modern generations, TMs are single-use consumables that can be crafted at a TM Machine.
* **Cost:** 0 Poké Dollars (**Not for sale**). It is acquired through exploration or by using League Points (LP) and Pokémon materials at a TM Machine.

## Effect & Mechanics
* **Primary Effect:** Teaches the move **Power Gem** to a compatible Pokémon. 
    * **Move Type:** Rock (Special)
    * **Power:** 80
    * **Accuracy:** 100%
    * **Effect:** Deals damage with no secondary effects. It is one of the few reliable, high-accuracy Special Rock-type moves in the game.
* **Battle Attributes:**
    * **Fling Power:** N/A (Technical Machines generally cannot be used with Fling in recent generations, or provide minimal power).
    * **Competitive Use:** **Highly Relevant.** Power Gem is a staple for Special Attackers who need Rock-type coverage to hit Fire, Flying, Ice, or Bug types. It is commonly seen on Pokémon like **Glimmora**, **Iron Moth**, and **Diancie**.
* **Evolution/Interaction:** This item does not trigger evolution. It is consumed upon use but can be remade if the player has the necessary recipe and materials.

## Acquisition & Lore
* **Availability:** In *Pokémon Scarlet & Violet*, TM101 can be found as a collectible item in the overworld (notably in rocky or cave-based biomes like **Alfornada Cavern**) or rewarded by Battle Court representatives. Once obtained, it can be replicated at any Pokémon Center's TM Machine.
* **Crafting Requirements:** Typically requires specific Pokémon drops, such as **Glimmet Crystals** and **Spoink Pearls**, alongside a set amount of League Points (LP).
* **Wild Held Item:** Not found on wild Pokémon. TMs are strictly utility items for the trainer.
