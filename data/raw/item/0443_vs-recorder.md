## Identity & Utility
**Vs. Recorder** is a **Key Item** categorized under **Gameplay** tools, introduced in **Generation IV (Sinnoh)**.
* **Description:** An advanced electronic device designed to record and playback battles conducted over wireless communication, Wi-Fi, or within dedicated battle facilities. It serves as a personal archive for a Trainer's competitive history. It is a **Key Item** that **cannot** be bought or sold.
* **Cost:** 0 Poké Dollars (**Special Acquisition**).

## Effect & Mechanics
* **Primary Effect:** The device allows players to save video replays of their battles. It specifically functions during wireless matches with friends, online Wi-Fi battles, and matches within the **Battle Frontier**. Beyond video, it acts as a digital logbook that tracks **Battle Points (BP)** earned and displays **Commemorative Prints** (medals/symbols) earned by defeating Frontier Brains.
* **Battle Attributes:**
    * **Fling Power:** N/A (Key Items cannot be held or thrown in battle).
    * **Competitive Use:** While it has no in-battle effect on stats, it was historically a **vital tool for the competitive community**. It allowed players to study their own playstyles, analyze opponents' strategies, and share "Battle Videos" with the community via a unique numerical code system.
* **Evolution/Interaction:** This item does not facilitate evolution. It is strictly a record-keeping device and does not interact with abilities like Pickup or Klutz.

## Acquisition & Lore
* **Availability:** Typically received as a gift from an NPC during the main storyline or upon first entering a Battle Frontier region. For example, in *Pokémon Platinum*, it is given to the player by **Looker** in Jubilife City. In *HeartGold/SoulSilver*, it is obtained when entering the Frontier Access.
* **Wild Held Item:** This item is **never** held by wild Pokémon and cannot be farmed; it is a unique, one-per-save-file item essential for tracking post-game progress.
