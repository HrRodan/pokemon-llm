## Identity & Mechanics
**Iron Tail** is a Physical Steel-type move introduced in Generation II.
* **Description:** The user swings its tail, which has been hardened to the density of tempered steel, to strike the target with massive blunt force. This metallic sweep is designed to shatter heavy armor and bypass physical defenses through sheer weight and momentum.
* **Effect:** Inflicts regular physical damage.
* **Secondary Effect:** 30% chance to lower the target’s Defense by one stage, making the opponent more vulnerable to subsequent physical attacks.
* **Targeting:** Selected Pokémon (Single).

## Competitive Profile
* **Stats:** Power 100, Accuracy 75%, PP 15.
* **Role:** High-Risk Wallbreaker / Coverage Nuke.
* **Usage:** Iron Tail is the "high-risk, high-reward" alternative to **Iron Head**. While Iron Head is preferred for its 100% accuracy and flinch chance, Iron Tail provides a significant Power boost that can be necessary for securing OHKOs against bulky targets. In competitive circles, it is sometimes colloquially referred to as "Iron Fail" due to its low 75% accuracy. It deals **Super Effective** damage to **Ice, Rock, and Fairy** types.
* **Attributes:** This move makes **Contact**, triggering effects like Rough Skin or Rocky Helmet.

## Distribution & Notable Users
It has an extremely wide distribution (404 learners), making it one of the most common physical coverage moves in the game.
* **Prominent Users:** Commonly utilized by STAB (Same Type Attack Bonus) users like **Aggron**, **Steelix**, and **Lucario** to maximize damage output.
* **Coverage Utility:** Famously served as the iconic coverage move for **Pikachu** to handle Rock-types. It is frequently seen on powerful non-Steel Pokémon like **Garchomp**, **Tyranitar**, and **Dragonite** specifically to counter the **Fairy-type** threats that would otherwise check them.
* **Acquisition:** Widely available via **TM/TR** in almost every generation, including its debut in Gold/Silver (TM23) and more recently in Sword/Shield (TR24).
