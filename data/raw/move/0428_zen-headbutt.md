## Identity & Mechanics
**Zen Headbutt** is a Physical Psychic-type move introduced in Generation IV.
* **Description:** The user focuses its mental energy and willpower into its forehead, surrounding itself with a telekinetic aura before ramming the target with a forceful blow. This combination of physical impact and psychic pressure can disorient the opponent, often leaving them unable to move.
* **Effect:** Inflicts regular damage and carries a chance to cause the target to flinch.
* **Secondary Effect:** 20% chance to **Flinch** the target.
* **Targeting:** Selected Pokémon (Single Target).

## Competitive Profile
* **Stats:** Power 80, Accuracy 90%, PP 15.
* **Role:** Reliable STAB / Physical Coverage.
* **Usage:** For many Physical attackers, Zen Headbutt serves as the standard Psychic-type **STAB** (Same Type Attack Bonus) option. While its 90% accuracy can be a liability compared to moves like *Psychic Fangs*, it remains a staple due to its wide distribution. It is particularly effective on Pokémon with the ability **Serene Grace** (like Jirachi), which doubles the flinch chance to a staggering 40%. It deals **Super Effective** damage to **Fighting** and **Poison** types.
* **Attributes:** This is a **Contact** move, meaning it triggers effects like Rocky Helmet, Rough Skin, or Static.

## Distribution & Notable Users
It has a very wide distribution (411 learners), making it one of the most accessible Physical Psychic moves in the franchise.
* **Prominent Users:** Iconic users include **Metagross**, which relies on it for STAB damage, **Jirachi** for "flinch-locking" strategies, and **Gallade**. It is also a signature thematic move for **Zen Mode Darmanitan**.
* **Coverage Utility:** Frequently seen on powerful Physical threats like **Salamence**, **Garchomp**, and **Iron Boulder** to provide a way to bypass defensive Poison-types (like Toxapex) or Fighting-types (like Iron Valiant).
* **Acquisition:** It has been widely available as a **TM** (Technical Machine) or **TR** (Technical Record) across multiple generations, including Sword/Shield and Scarlet/Violet.
