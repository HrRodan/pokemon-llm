## Identity & Mechanics
**Brick Break** is a Physical Fighting-type move introduced in Generation III.
* **Description:** The user attacks with a swift, powerful downward chop. This martial arts strike is forceful enough to shatter hardened defensive barriers, instantly neutralizing protective energy walls set up by the opponent before the impact lands.
* **Effect:** Destroys any active **Light Screen**, **Reflect**, or **Aurora Veil** on the target’s side of the field, then inflicts damage. The screens are removed even if the target is immune to Fighting-type damage (e.g., Ghost-types), though the damage itself will fail.
* **Secondary Effect:** None (100% chance to break barriers).
* **Targeting:** Single Target.

## Competitive Profile
* **Stats:** Power 75, Accuracy 100%, PP 15.
* **Role:** Utility Attacker / Anti-Screen Tech.
* **Usage:** While its base power is moderate compared to heavy-hitters like *Close Combat* (120 BP) or *High Jump Kick* (130 BP), it is chosen for its **Consistency** and **Utility**. It is the primary counter to "Screen Offense" or "Hyper Offense" teams that rely on *Grimmsnarl* or *Alolan Ninetales* to set up damage reduction. It deals **Super Effective** damage to **Normal, Ice, Rock, Steel, and Dark** types.
* **Attributes:** Makes **Contact**. This move is affected by abilities like *Iron Fist* (increasing power) and *Tough Claws*.

## Distribution & Notable Users
It has a **Massive** distribution (442 learners), making it one of the most widely available physical coverage moves in the franchise.
* **Prominent Users:** Iconic Fighting-type specialists like **Machamp**, **Lucario**, **Annihilape**, and **Infernape** utilize it for reliable STAB (Same Type Attack Bonus).
* **Coverage Utility:** Frequently seen on a vast array of non-Fighting Pokémon to provide essential coverage against Steel and Dark threats. Notable users include **Garchomp**, **Tyranitar**, **Dragonite**, and even **Mewtwo**.
* **Acquisition:** Extremely accessible; it has been available as a **TM** (Technical Machine) in every single generation since its debut in the Hoenn region.
