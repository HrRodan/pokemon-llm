## Identity & Mechanics
**Grassy Glide** is a Physical Grass-type move introduced in Generation VIII (Galar region).
* **Description:** The user harnesses the verdant energy of the earth to glide across the battlefield at blinding speeds, striking the opponent with a low-to-the-ground physical assault. While it appears as a standard attack, its true potential is unlocked when the field is lush with vegetation, allowing the user to outpace almost any opponent.
* **Effect:** This move deals regular damage. However, it gains **+1 Priority** if the field is under the effects of **Grassy Terrain**.
* **Secondary Effect:** None.
* **Targeting:** Selected single target.

## Competitive Profile
* **Stats:** Power 55, Accuracy 100%, PP 20 (Max 32).
* **Role:** Terrain-dependent Priority / Revenge Killer.
* **Usage:** In Generation VIII, this move was a "High-Value Nuke" with 70 Power; in Generation IX, it was adjusted to a "Priority Finisher" at 55 Power. It is the gold standard for **Grassy Terrain** teams, allowing slow, powerful attackers to bypass their Speed tier. It deals **Super Effective** damage to **Water, Ground, and Rock** types.
* **Attributes:** This move makes **Contact**, meaning it is affected by items like Rocky Helmet and abilities like Rough Skin or Flame Body.

## Distribution & Notable Users
It has a **Wide** distribution (116 learners), primarily consisting of Grass-type Pokémon.
* **Prominent Users:** 
    * **Rillaboom:** The premier user of this move due to its ability **Grassy Surge**, which automatically sets the terrain required for priority.
    * **Ogerpon (Teal Mask):** Utilizes its high natural Attack and Speed to pressure opponents, often benefiting from teammates setting terrain.
    * **Venusaur & Sceptile:** Iconic Grass starters that use it as a reliable priority option to pick off weakened threats.
* **Coverage Utility:** Frequently seen on non-Grass Pokémon like **Sirfetch'd** or **Dubwool** as a niche technical choice in doubles formats where Grassy Terrain is prevalent.
* **Acquisition:** Widely available via **TM/TR** in the Galar and Paldea regions, making it accessible to a vast array of physical attackers.
