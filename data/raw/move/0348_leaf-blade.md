## Identity & Mechanics
**Leaf Blade** is a Physical Grass-type move introduced in Generation III.
* **Description:** The user wields a razor-sharp leaf or a blade-like appendage to slash the opponent with surgical precision. This move is characterized by its swift, slicing motion, often associated with agile forest-dwelling Pokémon and master duelists. Its refined execution allows the user to easily exploit anatomical weaknesses in the target.
* **Effect:** Inflicts regular damage. This move has an increased critical-hit ratio (+1 stage, representing a 12.5% chance to crit normally).
* **Secondary Effect:** None (Higher Critical Hit Rate is a built-in mechanic).
* **Targeting:** Selected single opponent.

## Competitive Profile
* **Stats:** Power 90, Accuracy 100%, PP 15 (Max 24).
* **Role:** Reliable STAB / Consistency Option.
* **Usage:** Leaf Blade is widely considered the premier Physical Grass-type move due to its perfect accuracy and respectable base power. It is generally preferred over *Power Whip* for its reliability and over *Seed Bomb* for its higher damage output. It deals **Super Effective** damage to **Water, Ground, and Rock** types.
* **Attributes:** This is a **Contact** move and is classified as a **Slicing** move, meaning its power is boosted by 50% by the ability **Sharpness** (making it exceptionally lethal on Pokémon like Gallade).

## Distribution & Notable Users
It has a moderate distribution (38 learners), primarily focused on Pokémon with blade-like physical features or agile designs.
* **Prominent Users:** Iconic to the **Sceptile** line (its original signature move), and a staple for high-attack threats like **Kartana**, **Leafeon**, and **Virizion**.
* **Coverage Utility:** Frequently utilized by **Gallade** and **Iron Valiant** as a high-powered coverage option to dismantle heavy Water and Ground-type walls like Dondozo or Great Tusk. **Sirfetch'd** also utilizes it to complement its Fighting-type STAB.
* **Acquisition:** While originally a signature move, it became widely available as **TR50** in Generation VIII and continues to be accessible via TM in modern iterations.
