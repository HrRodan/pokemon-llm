## Identity & Mechanics
**Scorching Sands** is a Special Ground-type move introduced in Generation VIII.
* **Description:** The user blasts the target with superheated, molten sand. This maneuver combines the abrasive force of a sandstorm with the searing intensity of volcanic heat, creating a high-temperature projectile that can leave the opponent with severe burns.
* **Effect:** Inflicts regular damage and possesses high utility as a heat-based Ground attack.
* **Secondary Effect:** 30% chance to Burn the target.
* **Targeting:** Single Pokémon.

## Competitive Profile
* **Stats:** Power 70, Accuracy 100%, PP 10.
* **Role:** Utility Special STAB / Defensive Deterrent.
* **Usage:** Often referred to as the "Ground-type Scald," Scorching Sands is favored for its defensive utility over raw power. While it is weaker than Earth Power (90 Power), the 30% burn chance allows special attackers and defensive pivots to neuter physical wallbreakers on the switch. It deals **Super Effective** damage to **Electric, Fire, Poison, Rock, and Steel** types.
* **Attributes:** This is a non-contact move. Notably, it provides Ground-type Pokémon—who are usually focused on physical attacks or pure damage—a reliable way to spread status.

## Distribution & Notable Users
It has a wide distribution (105 learners), appearing frequently on Ground, Fire, and Rock-type Pokémon.
* **Prominent Users:** Commonly utilized by **Landorus-Therian**, **Garchomp**, and **Skeledirge**. 
* **Coverage Utility:** Frequently seen on Fire-type Pokémon like **Heatran**, **Volcanion**, and **Charizard** to provide essential coverage against Rock and Fire-type mirrors while maintaining the ability to cripple physical threats.
* **Acquisition:** Originally introduced via Move Tutor in the Galar region; it is widely available via **TM/TR** in Generation IX.
