## Identity & Lore
Remoraid (Pokedex #223) is the Jet Pok√©mon, a distinct Water-type entity introduced in **Generation II (Johto)**. While it appears to be a small, gray fish inhabiting the sea, its biological structure functions similarly to a firearm. Remoraid is renowned for its incredible marksmanship; it can forcibly expel water to strike moving prey with absolute precision from distances exceeding 300 feet. In the wild, it often exhibits commensal behavior, attaching itself to the underbelly of **Mantine** using its dorsal fin to scavenge for food and gain protection. Despite its small stature, it possesses a surprisingly diverse movepool, reflecting its design inspiration as a living weapon.

## Combat Profile
*   **Stats:** HP 35, Attack 65, Defense 35, Sp. Atk 65, Sp. Def 35, Speed 65.
*   **Archetype:** Mixed Glass Cannon / LC Wallbreaker.
*   **Type Matchups:** As a pure Water-type, it is **weak to Grass and Electric** moves. It **resists Fire, Water, Ice, and Steel** attacks.
*   **Move Selection:** Capitalizing on its equal Attack and Special Attack stats and unusually wide coverage, its optimal loadout includes **Hydro Pump** (high power STAB), **Ice Beam** (coverage against Grass), **Flamethrower** (rare coverage for a Water-type to hit Grass/Steel), and **Bullet Seed** (multi-hit coverage).
*   **Abilities:**
    *   **Hustle:** Boosts the Attack stat by 50% but lowers physical move accuracy by 20%.
    *   **Sniper:** Increases the damage dealt by critical hits from 1.5x to 2.25x.
    *   **Moody (Hidden):** Sharply raises one random stat by two stages and lowers another by one stage at the end of every turn, making it a volatile and unpredictable threat.

## Evolution & Biology
Remoraid is the first stage of a two-stage evolutionary line. It evolves into **Octillery** starting at **Level 25**. This transformation is notable for the drastic shift in morphology, moving from a fish-like "handgun" design to an octopus-like "tank" or "cannon" design. It belongs to the **Water 1** and **Water 2** Egg Groups.
